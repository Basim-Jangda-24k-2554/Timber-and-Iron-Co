Built by Hand in Austin

Timber & Iron Craft Co. was founded in 2014 by a collective of furniture makers and structural welders who believed that the best furniture doesn't come from a factory — it comes from a workshop where one person follows a single piece from rough lumber to final oil finish.

Every table, shelf, and surface we produce is cut, joined, welded, and hand-finished in our East Austin studio. Our steel frames are fabricated in-house — not subcontracted — so every weld that leaves the shop carries our mark.

We work with architects, interior designers, and homeowners across the country. No two pieces are identical, and that's the point.

Sustainable Sourcing

We source our timber from responsibly managed forests and reclaimed structures across the Southern United States. Every board is tracked from mill to finished piece, and our waste wood is repurposed into shop fixtures, sample blocks, and local donations.

Selective Harvest

We partner with mills that practice selective cutting — removing only mature trees to preserve forest canopy and soil health for the next growth cycle.

Reclaimed Material

A portion of every collection uses timber salvaged from deconstructed barns, factories, and water tanks across Texas — each with its own grain history.

Low-Waste Production

Our CNC nesting and hand-cutting workflow minimizes offcut waste. Remaining material becomes firewood, shop furniture, or is donated to local woodworking programs.
